What Lexoria actually produces

Lexoria takes messy consultation notes and turns them into a clean, attorney‑ready intake summary that you can review before saving or exporting.

Before: messy consultation note

“Client is a 35‑year‑old office worker. Says he was rear‑ended at a red light in Los Angeles back in May. Not sure of exact date, thinks it was around Golden Week. Car behind ‘was going too fast’ and ‘didn’t even brake’. Police came, but client doesn’t remember which officer. Neck and back hurt the next morning, went to ER, then chiropractor 2–3 times a week. Missed about a week of work, had to use PTO. Now wondering if it’s worth ‘doing something’ because the pain still flares up when he sits too long…”

Attorney‑ready intake summary
Facts

Client: 35‑year‑old office worker injured in a rear‑end collision at a red light in Los Angeles, CA.

Key facts: Low‑speed rear‑end impact; police responded; client treated at ER the next day and then a chiropractor 2–3 times per week for ~6 weeks; missed ~7 days of work and used PTO.

Client’s Claims

Ongoing neck and lower‑back pain, temporary inability to work, and a request to recover medical expenses, lost wages, and general damages for pain and suffering.

Potential Legal Issues
  • Liability for rear‑end collision (presumption of negligence; potential defenses unknown).
  • Causation between the collision and current symptoms.
  • Scope of recoverable economic and non‑economic damages.
  • Compliance with notice / limitations periods (exact accident date not yet confirmed).
Additional Information Needed
  • Exact accident date and location (intersection, direction of travel).
  • Police report number and any traffic citations issued.
  • Complete medical records and billing from ER and chiropractor.
  • Employment records confirming missed work, wage rate, and PTO usage.
  • Any prior accidents or similar injuries; photos of the scene, vehicles, and visible injuries.
Relevant Law / Case Law (when safe)

Where Lexoria can confidently identify a statute or well‑known case (for example, general rules about following distance and rear‑end collisions), it may surface it here. If not, it leaves this section blank instead of guessing.

What Lexoria does

Lexoria is an AI legal intake organizer. It automatically turns messy consultation notes into a structured legal intake summary:

  • Facts – key dates, parties, and timeline pulled out of messy notes
  • Client’s claims & goals – what the client is saying and what they want
  • Potential legal issues – issue candidates for attorney review (no legal advice)
  • Additional information needed – follow‑up questions and documents to request
  • Relevant law / case law (when appropriate) – only when the AI can confidently identify something real; otherwise left blank

Jurisdiction‑aware and practice‑area‑aware

Lexoria’s AI is configured for U.S. legal intake. It works in a few different modes so you can stay close to the matters you actually handle.

United States (General)

Use a general U.S. mode when you don’t want to lock into a specific state. The AI treats intake as U.S. matters without making state‑specific assumptions.

Optional state focus

When helpful, you can select a state focus (currently CA, NY, TX, FL). The AI will prioritize that state’s context while still deferring to your judgment.

Practice‑area guidance

Choose a practice area—such as personal injury, employment, family, contract, or real estate—so the AI can shape follow‑up questions and checklists around that area.
